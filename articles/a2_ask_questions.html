How to deal with insufferable questions from ignorant people


Let me describe a situation for you. You're having a lovely day. Things are going your way. Maybe you just got a raise or landed a contract you wanted.


And you unexpectedly run into an old acquaintance, let's call them P for person. You are happy to see P, it's been a long time, and you want to catch up. P also is pleased to see you and is eager to chat. What could possibly go wrong?


The conversation starts something like this:

You: Hey! Lovely to bump into you. How are you?

P: Hi! Great to see you. I'm good, just did A and B. How are you?

You: Wow, congrats on A and B. I'm going for C now and I'm very happy to have finished the D project.


Forgive my poor dialogue writing skills. But you get the point. It's a lovely exchange. And then this happens:


P: So when are you planning to have kids? / get married / why did you dye your hair green / or something else equally out of nowhere and inappropriate


Your heart sinks. There are no words to say. Well, there are a few words, like: Whaaaat? And some rude ones, I'm sure you know them.


In these situations, my head starts spinning a little bit. Why would they ask this? How is this related to what I said before?


These questions always have the assumption that whatever you are doing with your life is meaningless and not worth doing.


So what do you do if you don't want to be rude and do not want to participate in that sort of interrogation?


The answer is so simple it's ridiculous. Just ask P a question:

You: Why are you asking me this?


Do this with as much compassion in your voice as you can gather. That's it. Simple and yet so powerful.


With this one simple question, you turn attention away from yourself and onto P. Without being rude, mean, or angry. That clearly shows P that their question was inappropriate. There even might be something wrong with them because they asked.


But honestly, none of that even matters. What matters is that you don't have to explain or defend your life choices. The conversation is no longer about you. P can now try and figure out their way out. Whatever P does next, you can always ask more questions:


What do you want to talk about this? Is everything okay with you?


You can watch little clips with interviews on YouTube where actors shut down inappropriate questions in a graceful way. Notice that the recipe is always the same. They ask the interviewer: "Why are you asking me this?". That destroys the accusational-interrogational rhythm. And sounds very impressive.


Keep it friendly. A sarcastic tone might dampen your higher ground a little. Be kind and considerate, and also consider never talking to this person again.


I hope this helped. Thank you for your time!
